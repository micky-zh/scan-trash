# Valuation

## Before Valuation

Do not value a company until business quality, balance sheet safety, and earnings quality are understood.

If cash flow is unpredictable or accounting is unreliable, state `无法可靠估值`.

## Methods

Use the method that fits the business:

- Free cash flow discounting: stable cash-generative companies.
- Owner earnings: high-quality long-term compounders.
- Relative valuation: comparable companies with similar accounting and cycle position.
- Asset value: financials, real estate, resources, or liquidation-like cases.

## Scenario Requirements

Always include:

- Bear case.
- Base case.
- Bull case.

For each case, specify:

- Revenue growth.
- Margin or ROIC assumption.
- Reinvestment or capex needs.
- Terminal assumption or exit multiple.
- Resulting value range.

## Margin of Safety

Required margin depends on certainty:

- Very high certainty: 20-30%.
- Good but not exceptional: 30-40%.
- Material uncertainty: 40-50%.
- Cannot estimate: do not buy.

Use the margin of safety as a valuation discount, not as a slogan.

Core formula:

```text
Acceptable buy price = Intrinsic value × (1 - required margin of safety)
```

If using market cap instead of share price:

```text
Acceptable buy market cap = Intrinsic value market cap × (1 - required margin of safety)
```

Current margin of safety should also be shown explicitly:

```text
Current margin of safety = 1 - current price / intrinsic value
```

or:

```text
Current margin of safety = 1 - current market cap / intrinsic value market cap
```

Interpretation:

- Positive and large enough: price may be actionable.
- Positive but smaller than required: good business, but price not yet attractive enough.
- Negative: market is pricing above estimated intrinsic value.

Do not hide behind a single midpoint. If valuation is a range, show at least:

- Bear value.
- Base value.
- Bull value.
- Buy price under the base case.
- If needed, a stricter buy price under the bear-to-base range.

## Defensive Investing

The purpose of margin of safety is first to avoid permanent loss, not to maximize upside.

This should affect three things:

1. Discount rate / required valuation gap
- Better moat and cleaner governance can justify a smaller discount.
- Cyclical risk, leverage, weak cash conversion, opaque related-party transactions, or incomplete data require a larger discount.

2. Position sizing
- Wide moat, strong balance sheet, predictable cash flow, and sufficient discount may justify a normal position.
- Good company but thin margin of safety should usually mean `等待价格` or at most a small tracking position.
- Governance doubt or unreliable valuation means no position, even if the headline multiple looks low.

3. Risk control wording
- Explicitly say whether the main blocker is business risk or price risk.
- If the company is good but current price is not, say so directly instead of forcing a buy/hold narrative.

## Output

Valuation output must include:

- Current market cap and price if available.
- Key assumptions.
- Intrinsic value range.
- Acceptable buy price.
- Current margin of safety.
- Required margin of safety and why that level is used.
- Whether the conclusion implies no position, starter position, or normal position.
- Main reason the valuation may be wrong.
