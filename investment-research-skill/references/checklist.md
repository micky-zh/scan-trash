# Investment Checklist

## Business Quality

- Does the business have durable demand?
- Can the business model be explained simply?
- Is it a franchise business, commodity business, or hybrid?
- What gives it pricing power?
- Is the moat widening, stable, or narrowing?
- Are customers sticky?
- Can competitors replicate the advantage?
- Is the industry structure improving or deteriorating?

## Management

- Does management communicate honestly?
- Is capital allocation rational?
- Are buybacks done only when undervalued?
- Are dividends covered by free cash flow?
- Are acquisitions disciplined?
- Are related-party transactions material or opaque?
- Is management compensation aligned with long-term value?

## Financial Quality

- Does net income convert into operating cash flow?
- Is free cash flow positive through cycles?
- Is growth funded by internal cash generation or debt?
- Are receivables and inventory growing faster than revenue?
- Is leverage survivable under stress?
- Is there large goodwill or capitalized cost risk?

## Valuation

- What expectations are embedded in the current price?
- What happens if growth is zero for five years?
- Is the upside from business compounding or multiple expansion?
- Is the margin of safety large enough for uncertainty?

## Behavior

- Why do I believe I have an edge here?
- What would prove me wrong?
- Am I buying because the business is good or only because the price fell?
- Would I still want to own it if the market closed for three years?
